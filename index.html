About Me

My name is Katie Ediger and I am a User Experience (UX) Librarian at the Illinois Institute of Technology (IIT) in the Paul V. Galvin Library. I try to make the library's services and spaces more responsive to everyone's needs. As a librarian I spend a lot of my time helping people connect with and understand technology. At IIT, and previously at Northeastern Illinois University, I use Drupal to manage the library's web presence.

    • I have a degree in Anthropology from Beloit College and a Masters in Library and Information Science from Dominican University. After completing my undergraduate degree, I served as a Peace Corps Volunteer in Peru. I lived and worked in a tiny town in the foothills of the Andes, called Laquipampa. When I came back to the United States, I knew that I wanted a career where I was working directly with people in some sort of service environment. I worked in several nonprofit positions before I found my calling as a librarian.

    I got my start in the library field at Northeastern Illinois University (NEIU). I managed the library at NEIU's El Centro campus. I also supported a wide range of departments, including Social Work, Computer Science, and Geography and Environmental Studies, through library instruction and collection development.

    There are normally a lot of moving parts, as well as many stakeholders, with an institutional website. At NEIU I worked with other librarians to do user testing for the library website. Based on the testing, we made changes to better meet the needs of our university community. Watching people use the website and having them talk through their experiences was immensely helpful for seeing issues or problems with the site that we had never noticed before.

    I'm currently working on a second masters degree in information architecture and technical communication from IIT. I'm in the program to advance my technical skills and to more formally study user experience. I am also excited to hear and understand perspectives outside of the library world. When I'm not at work or in class, you will probably find me roaming around Chicago looking for good food or exciting alley finds.
